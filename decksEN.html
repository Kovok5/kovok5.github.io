Setup guide

Choosing Your
Deck

Your board size is important, as the wrong size makes everything harder. Here's how to find what's right for you and why it matters.

Parts of a Board

Deck

The wooden board. Usually 7 layers of maple pressed together. Width is the most important measurement when buying.

Trucks

Metal T-shaped axles that attach under the deck. They handle turning and take the impact of grinds. Match width to your deck.

Wheels

Made from urethane. Smaller and harder wheels (50–54mm, 99a+) suit street skating. Larger, softer wheels suit cruising.

Bearings

Sit inside the wheels. ABEC 5 or 7 is fine for most skaters. Keep them clean and dry — water is the enemy.

Grip Tape

Sandpaper sheet on the top of the deck. Keeps your feet in place. Replace when it gets smooth or peels at the edges.

Hardware

Eight bolts connecting trucks to the deck. Standard length is 2,2cm without risers. Don't overtighten them.

Width Guide

Deck width is measured in inches. It's the single most important number. When in doubt, most adult skaters ride between 17,78cm and 20,3cm.

19,6cm - 21cm The all-around sweet spot. Most adult skaters live here. 42 - 44-45 All-round

Where to shop

Best option

Local Skate Shop

The best place to buy a board, period. Staff actually skate, can set up your board properly, and will give you honest advice. Your money also stays in the local skate community. Visit one before ordering online.

Also fine

Online Skate Shops

Sites like Tactics, Skate Warehouse, and CCS carry legitimate gear from real skate brands. Fine once you know your sizing — not ideal for your first board, since you can't try anything.

Avoid Toy Store Boards

Boards sold at department stores or toy shops use cheap wood that warps and snaps quickly, and low-quality trucks that make learning much harder. Budget around €80–€140 for a real complete setup from a skate shop. It's an investment that actually lasts.
